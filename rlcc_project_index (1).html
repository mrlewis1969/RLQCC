Key Research Findings — All Sessions
📈

COVID doubled prices — and they've held at the luxury tier

All three target floor plans roughly doubled from 2020 to 2022. The broader La Quinta market has since corrected 13% from peak, but RLCC-tier homes ($1.4–1.6M) are holding near prior highs, consistent with historical luxury divergence patterns.

🏆

Encanto I is the sweet spot

Most-transacted model in your size range, purpose-built as 3BR/3.5BA with ensuite baths, on the Jones course fairways. At $573/sf the value case is stronger than Encanto II at a similar per-sqft price.

⚡

Inventory is thin — 1–2 Encanto I sales/month

The community does ~50–62 total closings/year. Your 3 target models combined account for ~22 of those. Having all 3 open is essential for 1031 execution within the 45-day window.

🏌️

Off-market deals are real here (~15–20%)

Ron Roisum's on-site office at RLCC sees transactions that never hit the public MLS. For a 1031 with a hard deadline, having an on-site agent is not optional — it's strategic.

🗓️

May–June is the optimal 1031 timing window

Peak season (Jan–Mar) offers only 1–2% negotiating room with high buyer competition. May–June gives you 3–4% discount room, motivated sellers, and decent inventory — without the thin summer pickings of July–September.

🏦

GFC took 13–16 years to fully recover

The Valley's deepest downturn dropped the broad median 52% peak-to-trough. Luxury golf community homes fell ~35–40% — better, but still severe. Recovery to prior peak prices took 13–16 years. The current correction (~15.7% from peak) shows none of the structural conditions that caused the GFC cascade.

📊

Luxury tier vs. broad market divergence is historically unprecedented

Indian Wells detached homes are 12.1% above their 2022 peak while Palm Springs is down 23.7% — a 35+ point spread never seen before. RLCC-tier pricing ($1.4–1.6M) is tracking with the luxury market, not the broad La Quinta correction.

💡

Price tier matters more than bedroom count for resilience

A 3BR home at $1.5M in RLCC behaves like Indian Wells luxury in downturns — not like a typical 3BR La Quinta home. Below $1M 3BR homes corrected 13–20% from 2022. Above $1.4M they've held. Bedroom count is secondary to price tier.

💰

Value anchor: $1.45M–$1.60M for Encanto I

At $573/sf average, a clean Encanto I should trade in this range. Below $1.40M likely signals deferred maintenance, limited view, or a motivated seller — worth understanding why before moving fast.

📅

Feb 2026 median was up 25.6% YoY

Redfin reported a $1.5M median for Rancho La Quinta in February 2026, up sharply year-over-year. Peak season (Jan–Mar) drives Q1 prices higher — timing your search to this window has tradeoffs.

Known Data Gaps — Fill With Agent
  • ⚠️ Pre-2023 floor plan breakdowns are estimated, not MLS-verified. Ron Roisum's office can pull actual historical comps by floor plan going back further — critical for establishing your 1031 replacement value basis.
  • ⚠️ 2026 individual sale records are not yet in public search results (MLS lag of 2–4 weeks). Redfin confirms 16 sales in February 2026 at a median of $1.5M, up 25.6% YoY — but floor-plan-level detail requires Ron Roisum or direct Redfin/Riverside County Assessor lookup.
  • ⚠️ Specific lot/view premiums are not captured. Golf course view, lake view, elevated lot, and cul-de-sac all command meaningful premiums within the same floor plan — not yet quantified in the analysis.
  • ⚠️ Golf membership availability and current cost not confirmed. Previously ~$105K initiation + ~$1,190/mo golf dues (695-member cap). Verify directly with RLCC before budgeting.
  • ⚠️ Terraza I vs. Terraza II split in transaction data is estimated. MLS often groups them. Ron Roisum can separate if Terraza II becomes a serious contender.
  • ⚠️ Furnished vs. unfurnished price premium not quantified. We know ~59% of RLCC sales are furnished. Whether furnishing adds 3–5% to sale price or merely reduces DOM has not been verified from the data.

    Tour RLCC before your 1031 clock starts

    Walk the community. Identify preferred streets, lot positions, and course views. Know in advance whether you want Jones or Pate course exposure, elevated vs. flat, north vs. south-facing. This lets you make fast, confident offers.

  • 4

    Confirm your Qualified Intermediary (QI) is ready

    Your QI must be designated before you close the relinquished property. If you don't have one, that's the most time-sensitive gap. Also explore reverse 1031 if timing is uncertain — you can acquire RLCC first, then sell the relinquished property within 180 days.

  • 5

    Anchor your value expectations before negotiating

    At $573/sf, an Encanto I should run $1.45M–$1.60M. Current market data supports ~3–4% negotiating room from list. Anything under $1.4M likely has a view tradeoff, deferred maintenance, or a motivated seller — understand why before moving fast.

  • 6

    Watch the macro signals in the recession file

    Rancho Mirage inventory is at 6.9 months of supply — the highest in the Valley. La Quinta attached homes are down 20.4% from peak. Neither is alarming yet, but both are worth monitoring over the next 60–90 days before you commit.
