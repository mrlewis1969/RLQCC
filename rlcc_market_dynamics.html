2018–2019 · Pre-COVID
Buyer's Market

Typical desert luxury pace. DOM 90–120 days. Sale/list ~95–97%. Absorption 5–7 months. Buyers had leverage; sellers priced down.

2020–2021 · COVID Surge
Extreme Seller's Market

DOM collapsed to 20–35 days. Sale/list hit 100%+ in 2021. Multiple offers common. Absorption fell below 1 month at peak. Buyers had almost no leverage.

2022–2023 · Rate Shock
Rapid Re-Calibration

Rate hikes cooled volume sharply. DOM bounced to 60–90 days. Sale/list fell back to 96–97%. Price reductions returned. Cash buyers cushioned impact.

2024–2025 · New Normal
Balanced / Slightly Buyer

DOM 35–91 days depending on pricing. 3–4 month absorption. ~3% avg discount from list. Sellers still well-positioned if priced right from day one.

Key Market Signals — Right Now (March 2026)
Based on most recent MLS, Redfin, and Coachella Valley market reports

💰 Average buyer discount is ~3–4%

Redfin reports La Quinta homes sell ~4% below list on average. At $1.5M ask, that's ~$60K of negotiating room — more than during 2021–2022 when homes sold at or above list.

📦 Inventory rising — now near pre-pandemic levels

Valley-wide inventory hit 3,313 units by mid-2025 (+34.5% YoY). RLCC-specific active listings estimated at 16–23 homes. The months-of-sales ratio was 5.5 valley-wide in June 2025, above the 4.0 historical average.

⏳ Luxury homes ($1M+) discount more

Coachella Valley market data shows homes over $2M averaged a 5.5% discount from list in Aug 2025 (vs 2% for $500–599K homes). Your $1.4–1.7M targets fall in the 3–4% range historically.

🌡️ Pricing discipline now critical for sellers

Sellers who overprice face longer DOM and price cuts. Only 11% of Coachella Valley homes sold above asking in June 2025, down from 15% the prior year. For buyers, this means more room to negotiate — but well-priced homes still move fast.

🗓️ 1031 Timing Insight

If your relinquished property closes in Q1 (Jan–Mar), your 45-day window lands mid-season when competition is highest. If it closes Q3 (Jul–Sep), you'll have more leverage but thinner choices. May–June is the sweet spot: decent inventory, motivated sellers, and 3–5% negotiating room.
